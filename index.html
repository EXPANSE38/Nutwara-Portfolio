      Self-Reflections

      ● SMART BOTTLE TRASH SORTER

      "At first, we focused mainly on the idea behind our first prototype rather than user behavior. That taught me that engineering isn't only about building something, it's about creating a system that people will actually use and benefit from. As we improved our project, we realized that encouraging better recycling habits was just as important as sorting trash correctly. This experience taught me the importance of understanding the problem from the user's perspective, working as a team, and continuously improving a design based on feedback."

      ● ATMOS DATA CHIANG MAI

      "Building my AQI website was my first major solo coding project, although I did get some help from my mentor along the way. I learned that debugging and patience are just as important as writing code. It was also my first time working with APIs, two of them, in fact. Which taught me how to bring real-time data into a website. I faced many challenges during development, but each one helped me improve my problem-solving skills. Overall, this project was an important step in my coding journey and gave me more confidence as a developer."

      ● MINECRAFT SURVIVAL MULTIPLAYER SERVER

      "Creating my own Minecraft Server was fun, Even though I enjoyed playing and hanging out with my friends on the server, I think I enjoyed the process of creating the server even more. It was my first time setting up a dedicated server, and I had a lot of fun learning how the coding and configuration worked. The most difficult part was port forwarding so my friends could connect, and it took me a while to figure it out. But I didn't give up, and I felt proud when everthing finally worked. This experience taught me patience, problem-solving skills, and it started a next step towards my coding interest."
